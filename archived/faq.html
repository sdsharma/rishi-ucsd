1) Is Project RISHI only for pre-med?


No, we encourage anyone who is interested in our cause to join and support us!


2) What are some ways I can get involved?


There are many ways you can be involved! We have positions open in our Fundraising, Marketing, and Project Planning committees. If you are interested in writing and in global public health, we are looking for more writers for our Blog. You can also sign up to be a volunteer at our events. If you have any questions or concerns, please send us an email! To learn more about these opportunities, come to our GBMs and Project Planning meetings.


3) I have an idea for Project RISHI but I am not on board. How do I convey my idea?


If you want us to know ASAP, please fill out the form below. Otherwise, come share your idea(s) at our GBM or other events!


4) I am pre-med. What are some opportunities for me?


Our trip to India in the summer is ideal for pre-meds. You will have the unique experience of wrapping wounds, performing basic procedures, and having hands-on clinical practice.


5) What are students’ experiences like in the India trip?


“Anandwan is truly an amazing place, and though it started as a home for the outcast and disabled, working there has shown me that its residents have more capacity, drive, and ambition than most people I've ever met.” - Mugdha Golwalkar


“When immersed in a community composed of patients with disabilities varying from broken backs to blindness, it is nothing short of amazing to learn that not a soul lies idle. Every single person willfully and (most importantly) happily finds a way in which to contribute and give back to the community.” - Anvay Ullal
