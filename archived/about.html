Project RISHI (Rural India Social and Health Improvement) is a non-profit organization whose mission is to promote the sustainable development and growth of rural Indian communities.

In partnership with local community members and social enterprises, we identify issues central to our target communities and provide the resources necessary to implement solutions through extensive field research and on-campus initiatives.




The first chapter of Project RISHI was set up at UCLA in 2005. Since then Project RISHI chapters have sprung up UC Berkeley, UC San Diego, UC Davis, UC Irvine, and Northwestern University.

The UCSD chapter of Project RISHI was founded in September 2009 by UCSD Alumni Nikhil Nadkarni ('11). We work in rural villages set up by Baba Amte in rural Maharashtra. Since our inception we have had two official trips to India during the summers of 2010 and 2012.




In 2011, Project RISHI was awarded the UCSD Organization of the Year from the UCSD Alumni Association.

UCSD Project RISHI students have also attended the prestigious Clinton Global Initiative University conferences in 2011 - 2014 gaining acceptance to these conferences based on our projects (known as commitments at the conference). This conference provides a great opportuntity for UCSD Project RISHI members to meet other motivated college students like themselves, learn about different types of commitments, network with people interested in similar work, and hear luminaries in their respective fields speak about all aspects of philanthropy.
















Based on the trip attendees' schedules, around ten students travel to India during the summer to get hands-on, invaluable experience at the villages. They live in a guest house at the village, and meals are provided in the village's communal eating area. In addition to overseeing and carrying out the various pre-planned projects, they are given to opportunity to wound-wrap at the leprosy hospital, teach disabled girls Bharatanatyam, work with blind and deaf children, help out in the pathology lab, work on agricultural sustainability projects, and volunteer at the children's school.


Students have to be ready to experience extreme tropical conditions such as rainfall, humidity, heat, and the presence of insects. Malaria pills are recommended. The total trip costs come to around $1700 USD including flights, domestic transportation, lodging, and food. No donor money is used to subsidize these costs, and each student pays individually for their trip. Flights are booked be our Travel Coordinator based on individual preferences.
