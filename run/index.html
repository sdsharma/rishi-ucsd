About Project Rishi.

Project RISHI (Rural India Social & Health Improvement) is a non-profit, volunteer-run organization consisting of undergraduates, graduate students, professors, and doctors from the University of California at Berkeley, Davis, Los Angeles, and San Diego. Our aim is to provide sustainable solutions for the basic healthcare, infrastructural and educational needs of rural Indian villages. In partnership with local community members and social enterprises, we identify issues central to our target communities and provide the resources necessary to implement solutions through extensive field research and on-campus initiatives.

The Impact of Your Donation.

  • We run a health campus educating children in India about Hygiene, Nutrition, and First Aid
  • We provide Age Specific Sexual Health Seminars that also include Bystander Intervention and Support Systems for victims of sexual abuse
  • We work on a variety of health-related projects each year such as Building Water Purification Systems, and Changing Social Perspectives on the Blind, Deaf, and those suffering from Leprosy
